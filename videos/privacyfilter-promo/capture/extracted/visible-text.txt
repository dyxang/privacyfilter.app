[h2] How Privacy Filter works
[strong] Load the model in your browser
[p] The openai/privacy-filter weights are downloaded once from Hugging Face and cached for future visits.
[strong] Run token classification locally
[p] Transformers.js executes the model on WebGPU when available, with WebAssembly as a fallback.
[strong] Review and copy
[p] Inspect the highlighted entities, copy the redacted text, or paste new content — nothing ever leaves your device.

[h2] Frequently asked questions
[span] Is my text uploaded to a server?
[p] No. Inference runs entirely in your browser through Transformers.js. After the initial model download from Hugging Face, your text never leaves the device.
[span] What kinds of personal information can it detect?
[p] Names, emails, phone numbers, addresses, account numbers, dates, URLs, and secrets such as API keys or tokens.
[span] Which browsers are supported?
[p] Chrome and Edge with WebGPU offer the fastest performance. Other modern browsers fall back to WebAssembly automatically.
[span] Why is the first detection slow?
[p] The first run downloads the openai/privacy-filter weights. Subsequent runs use the browser cache and are noticeably faster.
[span] Is Privacy Filter free?
[p] Yes. The page is free to use, and the underlying openai/privacy-filter model is published with open weights on Hugging Face.
[span] Can it replace a regex-based redactor?
[p] For most natural-language text, a contextual model catches entities that regex misses (multilingual names, free-form addresses) — though combining both gives the best coverage.
